sohyeon hwang

I'm a 2nd year PhD student in the Media, Technology, and Society program @ Northwestern University, advised by Aaron Shaw and part of the Community Data Science Collective. I also work with √Ågnes Horv√°t over at LINK :) My studies are generously supported by the NSF GRFP.

My research is inherently interdisciplinary, centered around understanding the landscape of governance in online spaces on the scale of multi-community + multi-platform analyses. Right now, I'm interested in the complexity of governing large-scale social computing systems, especially those following decentralized modes of moderation (e.g. Reddit, Wikipedia) that result in diverse notions of governance across subcommunities. You can read more about my projects and see publications over at the research page.

These days, I'm a co-organizer of a PhD student reading group focused on critical readings of technology/media — now under HCI+D — as well as the Metagov Seminar. My non-academic life often involves eating french fries, volunteering @ GirlForward, and shooting blurry 35 mm.

I received my BA from Cornell University, where I double-majored in Government and Information Science. I focused on revolutions and ideology (mostly focusing on 20th-century-ish Europe) in the former and data science and ethics in the latter — a slightly strange mix that brought me to where I am now. As an undergrad, I was fortunate to work with a variety of folks: the New York State Water Resources Institute, the Get Your Green Back Tompkins campaign, and Dr. Victor Nee over at the Center for the Study of Economy and Society. Between Cornell and Northwestern, I was an Emma Bowen Foundation Summer Fellow at Reveal / Center for Investigative Reporting on the Audience Team.
