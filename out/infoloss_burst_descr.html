Number of bursts and the relevant posts/research papers¶

We have two kinds of bursts. The bursts calculated as described in Cheng et al, which have the following conditions:

  • the daily number of posts is at least 10,
  • no less than twice the average number of daily posts,
  • a local maximum within a window of w = 7 days, and
  • the number of posts per day between the two adjacent peaks should drop below the half of smaller peak’s height

And then an "alternative" burst calculation. TLDR; the number of posts is heavily skewed to Twitter. We note that some media/platforms are much more amenable to post creation (e.g. Twitter has a simple retweet but Wikipedia and news sites have strictier editing or publishing processes); therefore, we modify the first condition (at least 10 posts that day) per platform. We take the log of the number of posts overall (in the entire Altmetric dataset? Sohyeon needs to double check this), divide that by the log of the total number of posts, and then multiple this fraction by 10 (the original threshold). The resulting number is the minimum number of daily posts for each platform.

  • Twitter - 9
  • Facebook - 8
  • News - 8
  • Blogs - 7
  • Wikipedia - 7

The difference between the bursts and alt bursts is not super substantial, however, alt bursts gives more comprehensive coverage of mentions while acknowledging differences in media/platforms.
