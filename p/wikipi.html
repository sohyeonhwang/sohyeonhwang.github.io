The decentralized use of (shared) rules

This project is an investigation into how autonomous community organizations that nonetheless share some core features (technical infrastructure, goals, essential norms) develop and implement rules to facilitate the self-governance of their spaces in heterogeneous ways. When communities are both interconnected and autonomous, what are the impacts and implications of the indepedent tracks of interpreting, implementing, and understanding rules for the system as a whole?
