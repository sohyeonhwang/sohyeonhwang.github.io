I study organization, governance, and collaboration in social computing systems, generally via multi-community/multi-platform studies to understand heterogeneous practices across diverse and overlapping online spaces.

+ more about my research

  • i tend towards interview studies and computational methods, but in principle i am fairly methods-agnostic;
  • in looking at social and organizational dynamics in online spaces, i'm often motivated by questions of accountability, the role of user innovation/participation, the relationship between heterogeneity and scale, and what behavioral patterns tell us about power + the effect of platform/technology design choices (and what we can do about these things);
  • right now, i'm especially interested in complexities arising in decentralized and polycentric modes of governance. e.g., how online groups variably develop, adapt, subvert rules + tools + platform affordances to govern themselves and the implications for platform-level regulatory effectiveness.

+ current project overview

last updated 02/2022
  • heterogeneous governance,
    studying variation in governance development and enforcement across communities on platforms like Wikipedia and Reddit. some of this work is supported by NSF grant IIS-1910202.
  • scale + distort,
    studying how scales affect information, content, and interactions e.g., information retention, the role of smallness, etc.
  • cdsc covid-19 digital observatory,
    I help maintain some data collection of covid19 mentions in online spaces and search engines with my research group.
