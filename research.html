A lot of my work is motivated by an interest in complexities arising in governance, such as how online groups variously interpret, innovate beyond, subvert, and co-opt platform constraints and allowances to manage themselves; what implications the diverse governing work of these groups has on platform-level regulatory effectiveness; and in what ways current ideas about 'online governance' might be inadequate.

I tend towards computational/quantitative methods, primarily computational social science, but I also do some more qualitative work and am in general fairly methods-agnostic.

If you're interested in collaborating or have any questions, please get in touch :)


projects

I have two main projects right now: (1) looking at distributed rule development and enforcement across multiple language editions of Wikipedia, and (2) studying information loss as content spreads across platforms. I'm also working on projects under NSF grant IIS-1617468 and helping to maintain some data collection for the CDSC COVID-19 Digital Observatory.
