[cv]

I study organization, governance, and collaboration in social computing systems, generally via multi-community/platform analyses to understand patterns of dis/similarity across heterogeneous and overlapping online spaces.

If you're interested in collaborating or have any questions, please get in touch :)

+ more about my research

  • i tend towards interview studies and computational methods, but in principle i am fairly methods-agnostic;
  • in looking at social dynamics in online spaces, i'm often motivated by questions of accountability, the role of user innovation/participation, the relationship between heterogeneity and scale, and what behavioral patterns tell us about power + the effect of platform/technology design choices (and what we can do about these things);
  • right now, i'm especially interested in complexities arising in decentralized and polycentric modes of governance. e.g., how online groups variably develop, adapt, subvert rules + tools + platform affordances to govern themselves and the implications for platform-level regulatory effectiveness.

current projects

heterogeneous governance,
studying variation in governance development and enforcement across communities on platforms like Wikipedia and Reddit

scale + distort,
studying how scales affect information, content, and interactions e.g., information retention, the role of smallness, etc.


I'm also involved with CDSC COVID-19 Digital Observatory where I help maintain some data collection, and work on projects under NSF grant IIS-1910202 on ecological approaches to online communities.
