I am a postdoctoral research associate at the Center for Information Technology Policy (CITP) at Princeton University, working with Prof. Andrés Monroy-Hernández in the Princeton HCI group. In my role, I'm also a Siegel Research Fellow.

My research asks how everyday people can have more agency in the technologies pervading our social, economic, and political lives. I primarily focus on the community as a valuable unit of organizing that can enable people to partake in meaningful governance decisions: collectively setting rules, norms, and tools that anticipate + respond to the opportunities and risks of technologies. I center my work at the community level because I think it raises hopeful and challenging questions of responsibility, autonomy, and scale. Answers to these questions orient how we might think about power, participation, institutions, and "good" sociotechnical design.

I move between mixed methods empirical work, theory, and design to understand cooperation + collective action, unintended consequences, and costs. My work is interdisciplinary. I am influenced by political science + government studies, sociology, human-computer interaction, science and technology studies, critical computing + data studies, and feminist theory. I often refer to institutional theory in the traditions of new institutionalism.

As a PhD student, I was advised by Prof. Aaron Shaw + part of the Community Data Science Collective (CDSC) in the Media, Technology, and Society program at Northwestern University. My studies were generously supported by the NSF GRFP. I am still collaborate with and am affiliated with folks in the collective :)
